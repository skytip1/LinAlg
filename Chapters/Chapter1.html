Matrices and gaussian Elimination

Before we start talking about Matrices and Gaussian Elimination you should be comfortable with the notion of vectors. The books first Chapter skims Over the concept of vectors pretty fast but you should really take a minute to know what a vecor represents. Below I have attatched some videos showing what a vector is.

Now that you have a solid grasph on what vectors are we should get into the algebra of it.

There are some simple opperations we can do with vectors. For instance take the vector $$\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix}$$ we can multiply any number to this vector and this gives the effect of "scailing" the vecor geometrically or in other words, $$c \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} cv_1 \\ cv_2 \\ cv_3 \end{bmatrix}$$
